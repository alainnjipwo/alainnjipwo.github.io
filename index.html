01 — Analog Design & EE Coursework

Core analog curriculum

The courses that shaped how I think about circuits — from small-signal models and noise analysis to high-frequency IC design and electromagnetic fields.

EE435
Analog VLSI Circuit Design

Basic analog integrated circuit and system design including design space exploration, performance enhancement strategies, operational amplifiers, references, integrated filters, and data converters.

Op-AmpsData ConvertersIntegrated FiltersVLSI
EE465
VLSI Design of Integrated Circuits

Digital design of integrated circuits employing very large scale integration (VLSI) methodologies. Technology considerations in design. High level hardware design languages, CMOS logic design styles, area-energy-delay design space characterization, datapath blocks: arithmetic and memory, architectures and systems on a chip (SOC) considerations.

VLSICMOS LogicHDLSoCDatapath Design
EE501
Analog & Mixed-Signal VLSI Design

Design techniques for analog and mixed-signal VLSI circuits. Amplifiers; operational amplifiers, transconductance amplifiers, finite gain amplifiers and current amplifiers. Linear building blocks; differential amplifiers, current mirrors, references, cascading and buffering. Performance characterization of linear integrated circuits; offset, noise, sensitivity and stability. Layout considerations, simulation, yield and modeling for high-performance linear integrated circuits.

Op-AmpsCurrent MirrorsNoise & OffsetLayoutVLSI
EE505
Data Conversion Circuits

Theory, design and applications of data conversion circuits (A/D and D/A converters) including: architectures, characterization, quantization effects, conversion algorithms, spectral performance, element matching, design for yield, and practical comparators, implementation issues.

ADC / DACQuantizationSpectral AnalysisDesign for Yield
EE508
Filter Design & Synthesis

Filter design concepts. Approximation and synthesis. Transformations. Continuous-time and discrete time filters. Discrete, active and integrated synthesis techniques.

Filter SynthesisContinuous-TimeDiscrete-TimeActive Filters
EE509
Mixed-Signal IC Testing

Introduction to mixed-signal IC testing; measurement uncertainty and test validity; IEEE standard test algorithms; high performance test and built-in self test challenges; new mixed-signal test algorithms and techniques to reduce data acquisition, relax instrumentation requirements, simplify test setup, improve test validity, and/or enable co-testing of heterogeneous functions.

IC TestingBISTIEEE StandardsMixed-Signal
EE532
Semiconductor Fabrication

Techniques used in modern integrated circuit fabrication, including diffusion, oxidation, ion implantation, lithography, evaporation, sputtering, chemical-vapor deposition, and etching. Process integration, evaluation, and final device testing. Extensive lab exercises building electronic devices, computer simulation for predicting processing outcomes, and recent advances in CMOS IC and MEMS fabrication.

CMOS FabricationLithographyIon ImplantationCVD & EtchingMEMS

03 — Computer Engineering

Programming & algorithmic foundations

Core computer science coursework underpinning my engineering practice — from object-oriented design and data structures to algorithm analysis and systems programming.

CPRE 185 & 186
Introduction to Computer Engineering and Problem Solving

Foundational introduction to computer engineering principles and systematic problem solving. Covers programming fundamentals, hardware-software interaction, and the engineering design process as applied to computational problems.

Problem Solving Programming Fundamentals Engineering Design
COM S 227 & 228
Object-Oriented Programming

Computer programming using objects as the mechanism for modularity, abstraction, and code reuse. Instance variables, methods, and encapsulation. Control structures, algorithms on strings, arrays, and lists. Recursion, searching, and sorting. Text parsing and file I/O. Interfaces, inheritance, polymorphism, and abstract classes. Introduction to the Design and Analysis of Algorithms

Basic techniques for design and analysis of algorithms. Sorting, searching, graph algorithms, string matching, and NP-completeness. Topics from advanced data structures such as balanced trees and hashing. Programming projects.

Linux Operating System Essentials

Introduction to installation, utilization, and administration of Linux systems. Linux Shell Scripting System Administration Networking Automation Security Open Source

Software Development Practices

A project-driven course covering the full software development lifecycle. Teams conceptualize, design, and ship a working application under real engineering constraints — learning risk management, rapid prototyping, and accountability along the way. Topics span requirements elicitation, software architecture, configuration management, testing, and professional ethics. Includes hands-on exposure to relational databases (SQL, OO modeling), networking fundamentals (DNS, HTTP, client-server APIs, sockets), and source control — all applied within a semester-long group project.

Agile / SDLC Team Project Software Architecture SQL & Databases Networking Version Control Testing & Reviews Ethics

04 — About Me

Beyond the bench

I'm Alain Njipwo, an electrical engineering student at Iowa State University with a focus on analog and mixed-signal IC design. I'm drawn to the parts of engineering that resist abstraction — where physics asserts itself and precision is non-negotiable. My coursework spans analog VLSI design, data conversion circuits, filter synthesis, semiconductor fabrication, and mixed-signal IC testing, giving me a strong foundation across the full analog signal chain.

Outside of engineering, soccer has been a defining part of my life. I have a deep passion for the sport — both as a player and as a fan — and follow the world's top competitions closely. When I'm not on the pitch, I'm watching, analyzing, and living the game.

I am equally passionate about cars, technology, and the outdoors. I approach each with the same engineering mindset — evaluating how well something executes its intended purpose, whether that's a Gordon Murray-designed race car, a high-performance PC build, or a well-tuned home audio system. When I need to reset, you'll find me on a trail.

I am actively seeking internship, co-op, and full-time opportunities in analog IC design, mixed-signal circuits, and RF systems. If you're working on something that demands precision, I'd love to connect.

Automotive Passion
My love for cars is deeply tied to my passion for engineering. Whether it is a machine engineered to dominate on a racetrack or a vehicle optimized to transport people with maximum efficiency and comfort, I appreciate thoughtful engineering in all forms.

Technologies
I am especially fascinated by how breakthroughs at the component and semiconductor level directly influence the capabilities of consumer products. Advancements in technologies such as 3D NAND continue to enable denser, faster, and more efficient storage solutions, driving the next generation of electronics and fundamentally shaping product design across the industry.

Beyond following the industry, I actively engage with advanced technology in my daily life. From building and upgrading my own high-performance PC with modern NVMe SSDs, next-generation RAM, and NVIDIA graphics hardware, to using devices like the iPhone and PlayStation 5, I enjoy understanding not just how technology works, but why it performs the way it does.

Leveraging my understanding of amplifier design, gain, and audio signal behavior, I built and tuned my home surround sound entertainment system for optimal frequency response and acoustic performance — applying engineering principles to create a listening experience tailored for both precision and immersion.

To me, technology is more than a profession; it is an ever-evolving field of innovation that I study, apply, and appreciate every day.
